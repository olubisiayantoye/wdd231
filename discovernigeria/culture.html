Culture

Nigeria's culture is a beautiful mosaic of traditions, music, art, and festivals that reflect its diversity. With over 250 ethnic groups, each has its unique customs and languages. The three largest groups are the Hausa-Fulani in the north, the Yoruba in the southwest, and the Igbo in the southeast. Festivals are a significant part of Nigerian life.

The Durbar festival in Kano showcases colorful horse parades, while the Eyo festival in Lagos highlights traditional masquerades. Traditional art, like the Nok terracotta sculptures and Benin bronze works, speaks of ancient civilizations and skilled craftsmanship. Music and dance are deeply rooted in Nigerian culture, with Afrobeat, highlife, and traditional beats captivating audiences worldwide. Nigeria's culture is truly a celebration of life and unity.
